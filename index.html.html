Why Vaccination is Important

Vaccination is a critical part of public health. It helps protect individuals and communities from serious diseases. Vaccines work by preparing the immune system to fight infections, ensuring that if exposed to the actual disease, the body can respond effectively.

Some key benefits of vaccination include:

  • Prevention of serious diseases and complications.
  • Protection of vulnerable populations such as infants and elderly individuals.
  • Reduction of healthcare costs associated with treating preventable diseases.
  • Contribution to herd immunity, which helps protect those who cannot be vaccinated.
